Treatments

Alcoholism (AUD)

Alcoholism, now known as the disease of Alcohol Use Disorder, (AUD) is a condition in which a person has a desire or physical need to consume alcohol, even though it has a negative impact on their life. This disorder is completely treatable with alcohol addiction therapy and treatment. A person can live a perfectly happy and normal life with this treatment.

Drug Addiction

Drug addiction is a disease characterized by compulsive drug-seeking answers use that is often impossible to control, despite its harmful consequences. There is a common misconception that those who use drugs lack morals and especially willpower and that they could stop their drugs simply by choosing them. In reality, drug addiction is a complex disease.

SCREEN ADDICTION

Addiction to cell phones is a relatively new phenomenon, but it is one of the fastest-growing addictions that we have ever seen. It includes internet addiction, screen addiction, game addiction, porn addiction, etc.




Gambling Addiction

Gambling addiction is often referred to as the “secret behavioral addiction” because it is soften unacknowledged. Just like all other addictions, the problem lies in the reward center of the human brain. A compulsive gambler is drawn to a high they feel when they place a bet which has parallel effects to the brain as substance use.

Neuropathic Pain

A shooting or searing sensation is a common description of neuropathic pain. It can go away on its own, but it’s usually a long-term problem. It can be relentless and terrible at times, yet it can also come and go. Nerve injury or a dysfunctional nervous system are common causes.

Psychologists use assessments to determine an individual’s emotional and intellectual levels of functioning. Reading, language usage, attention, learning, processing speed, reasoning, remembering, problem-solving, mood, personality, and other abilities are all tested.
